\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We believe that our algorithms must be viewed 
as the preferred default follow-based recommendation algorithm,
as opposed to the reverse chronological feed
that provides a poor user experience, favors hyperactive users
and incentivizes fast and massive content creation.
In particular, its practical transparency is a lot more limited 
than what might appear intuitively.
Unless a user follows very few accounts 
and can read through everything they post and repost,
the reverse chronological feed actually removes from their information exposure a lot of information,
and the user does not have a lot of mechanism 
to understand and control the influences they undergo.
