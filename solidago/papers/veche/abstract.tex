\begin{abstract}
    Some of the leading social medias' recommendation AIs 
    have recently been argued and legally judged
    to be part of an ``addictive design'',
    both in the US and in the EU.
    Unfortunately, given a lack of alternatives, 
    the only recommended alternative solution is too often the reverse chronological feed.
    Yet, while its transparency and simplicity indeed make it clearly less of a ``dark pattern'',
    this feed has plenty of undesirable properties.
    Namely, it provides a poor user experience, 
    favors hyperactive accounts, and incentives massive content creation.
    In this paper, we introduce \Veche{},
    an alternative flexible follow-based recommendation algorithm,
    whose principles are well-grounded in social choice theory.
    Namely, \Veche{} views a follow as an allocation of a unit voting right of influence.
    Followed accounts then act as \emph{counselors},
    who are asked to provide content recommendations.
    \Veche{} then constructs a feed out of the counselors' recommendation submissions.
    \Veche{} thereby provides a rigorous traceability of influence.
    We also present various generalizations that respond to practical needs,
    and implementations that we deployed on Tournesol and on BlueSky.
    Overall, we make the case that \Veche{} should replace the reverse chronological feed
    as the default follow-based feed.
\end{abstract}
